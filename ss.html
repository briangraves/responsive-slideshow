Why Would You Use Responsive Design?

  • One Content Base
  • One Code Base
  • Lines Between Device Categories are Becoming More Blurred
  • Feature Detection Allows for Better Means of Determining Context
  • Future Friendly - Sites Will Be Built to Naturally Adapt to New Device Categories and Screen Sizes

When Would You Use Responsive Design?

  • Client has significant non-desktop facing traffic
  • Most sites with a planned seperate mobile site are good candidates
  • Exceptions
    • User context is known and limited (aafpmax)
    • Need the ability to serve a completely different user experience to each device class

Changes in a Responsive Process

  • Internal Kickoff
  • Address All Channels
  • Content Strategy & Prioritization
  • Design Sketches & Prototyping
  • Visual Design
  • QA & Testing

Internal Kickoff

It is important for the Project lead, IA/UX, Creative, UI, and Engineering all to work collaboratively and be a part of the project from the earliest possible stages.

Address All Channels

Project planners and their team must begin to think in terms of single solutions that address all channels. A mobile first content strategy can help with this.

Responsive, done right, won't be specifically targeted at any particular class of device.

Content Strategy & Prioritization

Content Prioritization becomes more important when content doesn't have a fixed pixel location.

Tying content to proper metadata allows the content to be pulled in to any device experience.

Design Sketches & Prototyping

Responsive Interaction Patterns & Natural Breakpoints can be better worked out by turning low-fidelity designs into a functional prototype.

Visual Design

Designing around the content, natural breakpoints, and feature enhancements allows for better design of each state of a feature.

QA & Testing

It is important to understand that the design will be different from device to device. It's not about making everything look the same; it's about creating the optimal experience across all devices.

Device Testing becomes crucial.
